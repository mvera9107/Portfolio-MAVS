Redesigning the

A full redesign of how 35 million members earn, discover, and redeem rewards — transforming a fragmented loyalty system into a clear, motivating, and deeply personal experience.

Lead Product Designer

iOS · Android
Duration

Overview

Loyalty built over a decade.
Clarity lost along the way.

Starbucks Rewards is one of the most successful loyalty programs in retail — yet a decade of iteration had left the app experience fragmented. Reward tiers were opaque, redemption paths were buried, and the Stars-to-value mapping confused even power users.

Members were earning Stars they didn't understand, skipping rewards they'd never discovered, and churning at key loyalty milestones. The business impact was measurable: redemption rates sat 18 points below category benchmarks. The product wasn't broken. It was invisible.

"How do I know what my Stars are actually worth? I just see a number."

The Challenge

Five fractures in the experience

Before a single screen was sketched, we audited every touchpoint in the existing rewards journey — from app first-open through post-transaction. Five systemic problems emerged, each compounding the others.

  • 01
    Invisible progress. The Star balance was shown, but without context. Members couldn't answer "how many more do I need?" without mental arithmetic against a chart buried in Settings.
  • 02
    Redemption graveyard. The reward catalogue lived three taps deep. 62% of sessions ended without a single reward impression.
  • 03
    Tier opacity. Green vs. Gold was communicated by a card colour change. The benefits delta was never surfaced — members couldn't see why Gold was worth pursuing.
  • 04
    Contextual blindness. Notifications said "You have 150 Stars!" — but gave no guidance on what that unlocked or when those Stars would expire.
  • 05
    Personalisation void. Every member saw the same catalogue regardless of order history. A cold brew devotee was served Frappuccino rewards.

Before / After — Rewards home screen

The Approach

Research-led. Principle-driven.

We ran 24 moderated user interviews across four member segments, combined with 12 months of session analytics and a competitive audit of eight loyalty programs — Dunkin', Delta SkyMiles, Target Circle, and others.

Three recurring themes shaped everything that followed.

  1. 1

    Lead with progress, not balance

    Replace the Stars counter with a dynamic progress arc toward the member's next achievable reward. Context transforms a number into motivation.

  2. 2

    Surface rewards in the flow

    Integrate relevant reward suggestions into the order confirmation, home feed, and pre-checkout — not just a standalone catalogue page.

  3. 3

    Personalise ruthlessly

    Use order history and purchase signals to rank the catalogue. A cold brew member sees cold brew rewards first, always.

Key Experience Areas

Where the design landed

01

Rewards Redesign

The loyalty home was rebuilt around a single question members constantly asked: "What can I get right now?" A dynamic progress arc replaced the static counter, and a personalised reward queue surfaced the three most relevant rewards for that member, at that moment.

Decision rationale. Competitive analysis confirmed that best-in-class loyalty programs (Delta, Marriott) lead with progress, not balance. Members responded 3× more positively to "32 Stars away from your next reward" than to "You have 268 Stars" in A/B testing.

02

Home Redesign

The app home was redesigned to act as a contextual daily companion — morning coffee mode, afternoon pickup prompts, and reward nudges timed to each member's order patterns. The loyalty layer was woven into the home rather than siloed in a separate tab.

Home screen — Before / After

Decision rationale. Session data showed that members who saw a loyalty nudge on the home screen were 2.4× more likely to visit the rewards catalogue in the same session. Integrating the two surfaces removed the need for members to self-navigate to an awareness they didn't know to seek.

03

PDP Redesign

The product detail page was redesigned to make the Stars-earning moment visible and motivating at the point of decision. Each product now shows how many Stars it earns, how that contributes to the member's active progress, and whether a matching reward exists — all without disrupting the ordering flow.

Decision rationale. The PDP is the highest-traffic page in the app. Surfacing Stars context here required zero additional navigation — and gave every transaction an emotional layer it previously lacked.

Impact

Results, six months post-launch

The redesign shipped in phases across iOS and Android between September and December 2023. Six-month post-launch cohort data confirmed movement on every north-star metric — with some outcomes exceeding projections.

Reflection

What I'd carry forward

1

Clarity over cleverness

Every time we introduced a "smart" interaction pattern in testing, users asked for a simpler version. The biggest redemption lifts came from removing friction, not adding delight layers.

2

Fight for the emotional moments

The Stars celebration animation was a 2-week dev investment the team initially de-prioritised. It became the most-mentioned feature in CSAT open responses. Emotional beats compound; rational ones don't.

3

Design for the confused majority

Power users were the loudest voices in research. But the biggest business value came from fixing the experience for confused Aspirationals and passive Ritualists — who together represented 71% of the member base.

4

What I'd improve next

Deeper personalisation of the tier communication for the Gold → Platinum path, and a native nudge system that surfaces expiring Stars as a celebration moment rather than a system warning.
